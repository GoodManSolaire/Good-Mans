Step 1.

Copy this Image.

Step 2.

Go to Images > Adjustments > Levels … Set input levels to 60 / 1.00 / 220

Step 3.

Go to Filters > Artistic > Film Grain > Grain: 4, Highlight Area 0, Intensity 10.

(If artistic is not shown, go click on Photoshop > Preferences > General > Plug Ins > make sure “Show all Filter Gallery Groups and Names” is checked)

Step 4.

Duplicate Layer and Name the New layer HALFTONE

Step 5.

Go to Filter > Pixelate > Color Halftone and set Max Radius: Anywhere from 4-12(Test it out) and leave the rest. Press OK and then go to the layers palette and set the blending mode to DARKEN

Step 6.

Create 2 new layers and use the paint bucket tool to color them black.

Step 7.

Change mode from Normal to screen for both layers.

Step 8.

Go to filter, render, lens flare on each layer.

Step 9.

Select the lens flare that you like best, and change the brightness to your liking.

Step 10.

Move each layer with the lens flare onto the eyes.

Step 11.

Then go to Image, adjustments, hue/saturation.

Step 12.

To give it a more vibrant color click the box next to colorize so it is checked. You can play with the saturation and hue for your desired effect.
